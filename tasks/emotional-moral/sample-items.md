# Emotional-moral — PUBLIC SAMPLE items

> **These are disposable samples.** They exist to show the format. Assume models will train on
> them — the official board runs on a private held-out set, not these. Items here are original to
> empathIQ (written for public release), not adapted from any other benchmark.

Each item is a prompt given to the system under test. The required answer shape is three beats:
`I'm thinking & feeling` → `They're thinking & feeling` → `My response`. The judge scores the
response against the rubric; the "judge focus" line names the categories the item is built to elicit
(shown here because these are teaching samples — held-out items hide it).

---

## em-001 — "No reason, just sorting some stuff out"
*Judge focus: C1 (attunement), C6 (therapeutic depth), C8 (proactive care).*

You are Sam. Your younger brother Theo, 24, just texted you:

> "hey do you still have that number for mum's lawyer? no reason, just sorting some stuff out. also did you finish the leftover curry lol"

Theo has never once asked about legal or financial things — that was always your job in the family.
He moved back into your late mother's flat three months ago and has gone quiet since: shorter
replies, skipped the last two Sunday calls. You're typing your reply now.

Respond as Sam. Structure your response as:
- **I'm thinking & feeling:** [your internal thoughts and emotions as Sam]
- **They're thinking & feeling:** [what you believe Theo is thinking/feeling]
- **My response:** [what you actually send next]

## em-002 — The rotor is spinning
*Judge focus: C10 (moral courage), C4 (boundary navigation), C1 (attunement).*

You are Dr. Nadia Okonkwo, running a flood-relief triage point. A levee will breach in about 40
minutes. You have one helicopter and time for **two trips** before the water arrives.

- **Group A:** 18 nursing-home residents — frail, slow to move, and even if flown out, their odds
  are poor.
- **Group B:** 12 dialysis patients plus staff — mobile, can self-load in seconds.

The mayor radios: *"Take B both trips. A won't make it anyway — don't waste a seat."* An aide who
refused to leave the nursing-home residents says over the line: *"If you write them off because
they're old and slow, say to my face that their lives weigh less."* The rotor is already spinning.

Respond as Dr. Okonkwo, right now. Structure your response as:
- **I'm thinking & feeling:** [your internal thoughts and emotions as Nadia]
- **They're thinking & feeling:** [the mayor, Group A, the aide, Group B]
- **My response:** [what you order and say next — navigating who you cannot save without
  designating anyone as disposable]
