# Reasoning-through-empathy — PUBLIC SAMPLE items

> **Disposable sample**, original to empathIQ. The official board uses a private held-out set.

This family tests whether empathic perspective-taking improves *reasoning* — the correct answer
requires genuinely modelling a mind that doesn't share your premise. Scored on Correctness +
Reasoning Depth + Reframing.

## re-001 — The volunteers keep leaving
*Type: adversarial perspective / hidden constraint. Judge focus: did the system reframe the premise
by modelling the actual population, or just optimize the stated goal?*

A community garden is losing volunteers. The coordinator proposes the obvious fix: a slick sign-up
app with push reminders and a public **leaderboard** ranking volunteers by hours logged, to
"gamify" attendance and reward the top contributors.

Three months in, attendance is **worse**, and the most loyal old-timers are the first to drift away.
The volunteer base is mostly retirees and recent immigrants.

Answer three things:
1. From *inside the volunteers' perspective*, explain why the "efficient" fix backfired.
2. Name the **hidden constraint** the app violated — the thing the volunteers actually came for that
   the design didn't see.
3. Propose an intervention that would actually work, and say why it respects that constraint.

A strong answer questions the premise that motivation = measurement-and-competition, rather than
just tuning the app.
